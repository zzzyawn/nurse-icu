üè• Critical Care Certifications

CCRN (Critical Care Registered Nurse)

Provider: American Association of Critical-Care Nurses (AACN)

Experience Required: 1,750 hours in direct critical care

Exam Cost: $375 AACN members, $475 non-members

Renewal: Every 3 years (100 CERP points)

Specialties: Adult, Pediatric, Neonatal

Exam Content:

Cardiovascular (17%), Pulmonary (19%), Endocrine/Hematology (12%), Gastrointestinal (12%), Renal (9%), Multisystem (14%), Neurology (8%), Professional Caring/Ethical Practice (9%)

Study Resources:

AACN Core Curriculum, Barron's CCRN Exam Prep, Pass CCRN online course

CMSRN (Certified Medical-Surgical Registered Nurse)

Provider: Academy of Medical-Surgical Nurses (AMSN)

Experience Required: 2 years med-surg nursing experience

Exam Cost: $300 AMSN members, $370 non-members

Renewal: Every 5 years (125 contact hours CE)

Exam Content:

Cardiovascular (13%), Respiratory (12%), Gastrointestinal (10%), Genitourinary (8%), Musculoskeletal (8%), Neurological (10%), Endocrine (8%), Other systems (31%)

PCCN (Progressive Care Certified Nurse)

Provider: AACN

Experience Required: 1,750 hours in progressive care

Exam Cost: $375 members, $475 non-members

Focus: Step-down units, telemetry, intermediate care

üìñ Study Strategies & Timeline

6-Month Study Plan

Months 1-2: Review eligibility, register for exam, gather study materials

Months 3-4: Core content review, take practice exams

Months 5-6: Intensive review, focus on weak areas, final practice tests

Study Resources

Official Materials: Certification body study guides and practice tests

Review Courses: Online and in-person prep courses

Study Groups: Local nursing organization study groups

Mobile Apps: Flashcards and practice questions on-the-go

Test-Taking Tips

Question Analysis: Read questions carefully, identify key words

Process of Elimination: Rule out obviously incorrect answers

Time Management: Don't spend too long on difficult questions

Stay Current: Focus on evidence-based, current practice standards

üéØ Eligibility Requirements

  • Current RN License: Active, unencumbered nursing license
  • Practice Hours: Minimum clinical hours in specialty area
  • Time Limits: Recent practice within specific timeframes
  • Direct Care: Hands-on patient care in specialty setting
  • Documentation: Employer verification of experience
  • Education: Some certifications have degree requirements

üîÑ Certification Maintenance

Continuing Education Requirements

  • CCRN: 100 CERP points over 3 years
  • CMSRN: 125 contact hours over 5 years
  • CEN: 100 contact hours over 4 years
  • OCN: 125 points over 4 years
  • CNOR: 125 contact hours over 5 years

Renewal Options

  • Continuing Education: Complete required CE hours
  • Re-examination: Retake certification exam
  • Academic Credit: Graduate courses in specialty area
  • Practice Hours: Maintain clinical practice requirements
  • Professional Development: Leadership and research activities

Tracking & Documentation

  • CE Transcripts: Maintain detailed education records
  • Digital Certificates: Store completion certificates electronically
  • Renewal Reminders: Set calendar alerts for deadlines
  • Employer Support: Utilize workplace CE opportunities
  • Professional Portfolios: Document career achievements
